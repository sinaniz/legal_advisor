Health Care Directive Template

This is a basic Health Care Directive (also known as an Advance Directive or Living Will) template. This document allows you to express your wishes regarding medical treatment if you become unable to communicate your decisions. Review all sections carefully and fill in your information. Once ready, you can download the template. Please note that this template is for reference only and should be reviewed by a legal professional before finalizing, as requirements vary by state/jurisdiction.

HEALTH CARE DIRECTIVE

I, [Your Full Legal Name], being of sound mind, willfully and voluntarily make this Health Care Directive to express my wishes regarding future health care decisions in the event that I am unable to make those decisions myself.

PART I: DESIGNATION OF HEALTH CARE AGENT

I hereby appoint the following person as my Health Care Agent to make health care decisions for me if I become incapable of making my own decisions:

Primary Health Care Agent:
Name: [Agent's Full Name]
Address: [Agent's Address]
Phone: [Agent's Phone Number]
Relationship: [Relationship to You]

Alternate Health Care Agent: (If my primary agent is unwilling, unable, or unavailable to act)
Name: [Alternate Agent's Full Name]
Address: [Alternate Agent's Address]
Phone: [Alternate Agent's Phone Number]
Relationship: [Relationship to You]

PART II: POWERS OF HEALTH CARE AGENT

I grant my Health Care Agent full authority to make all health care decisions for me if I am unable to make decisions or communicate my wishes. This includes, but is not limited to:

  • Consenting to, refusing, or withdrawing consent to any type of medical care, treatment, surgical procedures, diagnostic procedures, medication, and the use of mechanical or other procedures affecting any bodily function;
  • Requesting, receiving, and reviewing any information, verbal or written, regarding my physical or mental health, including medical and hospital records;
  • Executing any documents that may be required to carry out my health care decisions;
  • Deciding on admission to or discharge from any hospital, hospice, nursing home, or other health care facility;
  • Hiring and firing medical personnel to provide care to me;
  • Authorizing the transfer of my care to another physician or health care facility as appropriate.

PART III: STATEMENT OF DESIRES, SPECIAL PROVISIONS, AND LIMITATIONS

If I am in a terminal condition or permanent unconscious state and there is no reasonable expectation of my recovery, I direct that I be allowed to die naturally, without life-sustaining procedures, with only the administration of medication, sustenance, or procedures deemed necessary to provide comfort care or alleviate pain.

PART V: GENERAL PROVISIONS

Severability: If any provision in this document is found to be invalid or unenforceable, this does not affect the validity of the remainder of this document.

Revocation: I understand that I may revoke this Health Care Directive at any time by destroying this document, by directing someone else to destroy it in my presence, by signing a written and dated statement of my intent to revoke, or by stating that I wish to revoke it in the presence of a witness who is at least 18 years of age and who signs and dates a statement confirming that such an oral expression of intent was made.

Prior Directives: I revoke any prior health care directive or living will.
